# User Personas

## Persona 1: Beginner Aquascaper

### Profile

**Name:** Bo Rivera <br />

Bo is new to planted aquariums and has limited experience with aquatic plants. They may have kept fish before, but this is their first attempt at creating a visually designed aquascape. <br />

Bo spends considerable time watching aquascaping videos, browsing online forums, and admiring photographs of professional aquascapes. However, they often feel overwhelmed by conflicting advice and the large number of available plants and materials. <br />

Before making purchases, Bo wants reassurance that their choices will work together and produce an attractive result.

### Typical Tank Size

- 10-20 gallon aquarium
- Most comfortable with a 20-gallon long or standard 20-gallon tank
- Views larger aquariums as intimidating and expensive

### Design Goals

- Create a beautiful first planted aquarium
- Avoid costly mistakes
- Learn basic aquascaping principles
- Select plants that are likely to succeed
- Understand where to place foreground, midground, and background plants
- Build confidence in the hobby

### Success Looks Like

Bo successfully creates a healthy planted tank that resembles the layout they envisioned and gains enough confidence to attempt a more ambitious aquascape in the future.

## Persona 2: Intermediate Aquascaper

### Profile

**Name:** Niko Hernández <br />

Niko has maintained planted aquariums for several years and understands the fundamentals of lighting, fertilization, and plant care. <br />

They have completed multiple aquascapes and are comfortable experimenting with different plant species and layout styles. However, they still encounter challenges when estimating how plants will mature and interact over time. <br />

Niko often rescapes existing aquariums and enjoys refining layouts to achieve a more polished appearance.

### Typical Tank Size

- 20-75 gallon aquarium
- Most comfortable with tanks between 40 and 75 gallons
- Sees larger tanks as an opportunity rather than a challenge

### Design Goals

- Improve composition and visual balance
- Explore different aquascaping styles
- Predict plant growth more accurately
- Reduce the need for major rescapes
- Optimize plant placement before purchasing
- Create aquascapes that remain attractive as they mature

### Success Looks Like

Niko creates aquascapes that require fewer corrections after planting and more closely match their original vision six months later.

---

## Persona 3: Advanced Aquascaper

### Profile

**Name:** Ari Nakamura

Ari is an experienced aquascaper with extensive knowledge of plants, layout techniques, and aquarium maintenance. <br />

They regularly study competition aquascapes, analyze design trends, and seek continuous improvement. They are comfortable working with demanding plant species and complex hardscape arrangements. <br />

Rather than needing guidance, Ari seeks tools that accelerate planning, experimentation, and design iteration.

### Typical Tank Size

- 60-180 gallon aquarium
- Comfortable working with both small showcase tanks and large display aquariums
- Selects tank size based on design goals rather than experience level

### Design Goals

- Refine layouts before implementation
- Prototype multiple design concepts efficiently
- Predict long-term visual balance
- Optimize hardscape composition
- Prepare competition-level aquascapes
- Push creative boundaries while minimizing costly revisions

### Success Looks Like

Ari can evaluate multiple layout concepts digitally, identify potential design issues before setup, and execute a highly polished aquascape that closely aligns with their artistic vision.

---

## What Each Persona Is Trying To Avoid

- **Bo (Beginner)** fears failure.
- **Niko (Intermediate)** fears wasted effort.
- **Ari (Advanced)** fears wasted time.
